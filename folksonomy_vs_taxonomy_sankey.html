①
Taxonomy owns technical structure
The only cluster where MobyGames dominates is Camera, Controls & Perspective (71% MobyGames). Taxonomy excels at classifying technical apparatus — 1st-person, isometric, behind view. Every other cluster is 75–99% Steam.
②
88% of Steam tags have no MobyGames match
396 of 450 Steam tags — Cozy, Wholesome, Boomer Shooter, Roguelite — exist nowhere in MobyGames. Communities coin language for experiences taxonomy never anticipated.
③
Affective tagging is folksonomy-exclusive
Tone & Mood is 99% Steam, 1% MobyGames. Funny, Dark, Cute, Relaxing, Emotional classify how a game feels. Taxonomy says what something is; folksonomy says what it's like.
④
Adult & Horror: absent by institutional choice
Adult & Mature Content (88% Steam) and Horror (91% Steam) are nearly invisible in MobyGames. This mirrors library collection policy: institutional taxonomies reflect what curators choose to make discoverable — and what they deliberately exclude (Svenonius, 2000).
